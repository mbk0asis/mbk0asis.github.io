Our Technology

HCR-Based Spatial Omics

Our innovative approach combines Hybridization Chain Reaction (HCR) amplification with peptide nucleic acids (PNAs) to achieve unprecedented single-cell spatial resolution. This breakthrough technology enables highly specific detection of molecular targets within tissue contexts.

Peptide Nucleic Acids

Superior binding affinity and stability compared to traditional DNA probes

HCR Amplification

Signal amplification without enzymatic reactions for enhanced sensitivity

Precision Detection Mechanism

Our HCR-based approach utilizes carefully designed hairpin probes that remain kinetically trapped until activated by specific target sequences. This ensures minimal background noise and exceptional specificity in complex tissue environments.

Key Technical Features:

  • Isothermal amplification process
  • Multiplexed target detection
  • Preserved tissue morphology
  • Quantitative signal output

Competitive Advantages

High Specificity

Peptide nucleic acids provide superior target binding specificity, reducing cross-reactivity and false positive signals.

Low Off-Target Effects

Engineered probe design minimizes non-specific binding, ensuring clean and reliable detection results.

Enhanced Sensitivity

HCR amplification provides robust signal enhancement while maintaining spatial resolution integrity.

PNA Structural Advantage

Neutral backbone eliminates electrostatic repulsion,
enabling stronger and more specific hybridization.
